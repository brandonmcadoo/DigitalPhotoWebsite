About Me

I am a photographer drawn to energy, atmosphere, and motion. A lot of my work focuses on moments that feel alive whether they are fast, chaotic, controlled, or quiet. I am interested in how a single frame can hold tension and emotion and how everyday scenes can become powerful when they are photographed with intention.

Instead of building everything from scratch in a studio, I often start with what is already happening around me. I look for movement, small shifts in light, and brief points where time feels like it slows down or rushes forward. My goal is to take those real moments and translate their emotion into images that feel immediate and honest.

Across different assignments and series I explore how light, space, and timing shape the mood of a photograph. Some projects are more abstract and focus on texture or form, while others center on people, still life, or motion. Together they reflect the way I see the world through the camera.

Photography lets me do more than show what something looks like. It gives me a way to share what a moment felt like to stand there and watch it happen. That emotional impact is what drives my work and connects all of the images in this portfolio.

Focus Areas

  • Action and movement
  • Everyday scenes with strong atmosphere
  • Portraiture and still life
  • Abstraction through light and texture

Currently Exploring

  • How motion changes the feeling of a space
  • Timing and near missed moments
  • Contrast between calm and chaos in a single series
